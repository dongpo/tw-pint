= Relationship between Peppol and Taiwan eGUI

== Architectural distinction

[cols="1,2,2", options="header"]
|===
|Layer |Peppol / TW-PINT |Taiwan eGUI

|Primary role
|Business document exchange
|Domestic tax reporting and invoice compliance

|Network role
|Interoperable international exchange
|Domestic government reporting platform

|Core object
|PINT invoice / UBL invoice
|Government Uniform Invoice record

|Scope
|B2B exchange and interoperability
|Tax reporting, archival, and domestic compliance
|===

== TW-PINT as the interoperability bridge

[source,text]
----
Seller ERP
   ↓
TW-PINT Invoice
   ↓
Peppol Access Point
   ↓
Foreign or domestic trading partner

Optional local path:
TW-PINT Invoice
   ↓
eGUI Gateway
   ↓
Taiwan eGUI / tax reporting process
----

TW-PINT provides the semantic and validation layer that enables invoice data to be exchanged internationally while preserving information needed for Taiwan local mapping.

== What TW-PINT does not replace

TW-PINT does not replace Taiwan eGUI legal reporting obligations, MOF platform processes, domestic consumer eGUI functions, carrier/donation/lottery functions, or legal tax determination.

== eGUI bridge principle

The eGUI bridge should map TW-PINT invoice information into local eGUI-related processes where required. The bridge should not create new Peppol semantics when existing PINT/UBL structures are sufficient.
