= 3. Tax Information


Taiwan imposes Business Tax under the Value-added and Non-value-added Business Tax Act. For most B2B transactions covered by TW-PINT, Business Tax is collected through the Uniform Invoice (GUI/eGUI) system and is represented as a consumption tax applied to the sale of goods and services.

TW-PINT does not redefine Taiwan tax legislation. Instead, it provides a mapping between Taiwan Business Tax information and the tax structures defined by PINT and UBL. The objective is to support cross-border interoperability while maintaining compatibility with Taiwan electronic invoice requirements.

== 3.1. Taiwan Business Tax

Business Tax is the principal indirect tax represented in TW-PINT. For the purposes of TW-PINT v0.1, only mainstream invoice scenarios supported by Taiwan electronic invoices are considered.

The legal basis for Business Tax (BT) reporting remains Value-added and Non-value-added Business Tax Act and Regulations Governing the Use of Uniform Invoices. TW-PINT is an interoperability specification and does not replace statutory reporting obligations.

=== 3.1.1. Overview of Taiwan Business Tax

Taiwan Business Tax distinguishes several taxation categories. The most common categories are:


[cols="1,2,1,1", options="header"]
|===
|Taiwan Tax Category|Tax Percentage |Description |Code

|Taxable (應稅) |5% |Taxable transactions refers to supplies of goods or services that are subject to Taiwan Business Tax at the applicable statutory rate. For standard taxable transactions, the supplier is required to calculate, charge, and report Business Tax on the taxable amount in accordance with Taiwan tax regulations. Businesses making taxable supplies may generally deduct eligible input Business Tax incurred on related purchases and expenses, subject to applicable tax laws and regulations. | S
|Zero-tax-rate (零稅率) |0% |Zero-rated transactions refers to supplies that are subject to Taiwan Business Tax but are taxed at a rate of zero percent (0%). Common examples include qualifying export goods and services as specified by Taiwan tax regulations.
Unlike exempt transactions, zero-rated transactions remain taxable supplies under the Business Tax system. Businesses making zero-rated supplies may be entitled to deduct or claim refunds of eligible input Business Tax incurred on related purchases and expenses, subject to applicable tax laws and regulations. |Z
|Tax-exempt (免稅) |0% |Tax-exempt transactions refer  to supplies of goods or services that are exempt from Taiwan Business Tax under applicable laws and regulations. For exempt transactions, no Business Tax is charged on the sale. In general, businesses making exempt supplies are not entitled to deduct the input Business Tax incurred on related purchases and expenses, except where otherwise provided by law. |E 
|===

TW-PINT v0.1 supports standard taxable transactions and zero-rated transactions. Exempt and special tax scenarios are outside the scope of this version.

=== 3.1.3. Taxable and Zero-Rated Transactions

Taxable transactions are represented using category S and are subject to Business Tax at the applicable rate.

Zero-rated transactions are represented using category Z and include transactions that remain taxable under Taiwan law but are subject to a zero-percent rate, such as qualifying export transactions.

The distinction between zero-rated and exempt transactions is important because only zero-rated transactions remain taxable under Taiwan Business Tax legislation.

=== 3.1.6. Calculation of Business Tax

The tax amount shall be calculated from the taxable amount and the applicable tax percentage.

For standard taxable transactions: Tax Amount = Taxable Amount × 5%

The sum of tax category amounts shall equal the document-level tax total.

The sum of taxable amount and tax amount shall equal the tax-inclusive total amount, subject to the rounding rules specified in Chapter 4.

=== 3.1.7.Foreign Currency Invoices

TW-PINT permits invoices issued in currencies other than Taiwanese dollar (TWD).

Where Taiwan tax reporting requires amounts to be expressed in TWD, conversion and reporting requirements remain governed by Taiwan tax regulations and eGUI implementation rules.

TW-PINT does not prescribe a mandatory foreign-exchange conversion methodology in v0.1.

=== 3.1.8. Credit Notes and Tax Corrections

Tax adjustments shall be represented using Credit Notes and shall reference the original invoice.

The processing of invoice cancellation, allowance, correction, or other eGUI-specific procedures remains the responsibility of Taiwan eGUI systems and associated gateway implementations.

TW-PINT does not introduce Taiwan-specific tax correction document types.
